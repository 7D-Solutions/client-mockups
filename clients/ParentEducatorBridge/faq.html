About the Course

What is the "Bridging the Gap" course?

‚ñº

"Bridging the Gap" is a 6-week comprehensive course designed to help parents and educators understand what children with autism need BEFORE school can work effectively. Created by a school psychologist and Licensed Professional Counselor with 35+ years of experience, this course bridges the communication gap between families and schools.

The course covers five critical foundation skills, navigating the IEP process, understanding special education law, and building effective partnerships with your child's school team.

Who is this course for?

‚ñº

This course is designed for:

  • Parents of children with autism who want to understand the special education system and learn how to teach foundational skills at home
  • Educators and school professionals seeking to understand the parent perspective and improve collaboration
  • Speech therapists, occupational therapists, and BCBAs who work with families
  • Anyone who supports children with autism and wants evidence-based, practical strategies

The course is especially valuable if you've felt frustrated by IEP meetings, confused about what services your child should receive, or overwhelmed by where to start.

How long is the course?

‚ñº

The course runs for 6 weeks, with each week covering a specific topic:

  • Week 1: Understanding the special education landscape
  • Week 2: Foundation skills your child needs
  • Week 3: Boundaries, safety, and saying "no"
  • Week 4: Understanding behavior and preventing meltdowns
  • Week 5: Navigating the IEP process
  • Week 6: Building the home-school partnership

Each session is 90 minutes for live formats, or 60-90 minute pre-recorded modules for self-paced learning.

  • In-Person Workshop ($597): Weekly 2-hour sessions with hands-on practice, role-playing, and local networking (10-20 families per cohort)

All formats include downloadable materials and resources.

Do I need any special materials?

‚ñº

No special materials are required! Everything you need is provided:

  • Downloadable workbooks and handouts
  • Visual support templates (PECS, visual schedules)

For Parents

My child was just diagnosed‚Äîis this course right for me?

‚ñº

Yes‚Äîthis is the perfect time! Early intervention is critical, and understanding what your child needs NOW (before school starts or in the early years) will set you up for long-term success.

This course will help you:

  • Understand what to prioritize in the early years
  • Learn the foundational skills to teach at home
  • Navigate early intervention services and school transitions
  • Build a strong foundation before IEP meetings become complex

Many parents say, "I wish I had taken this course right after diagnosis."

Will this course help me at IEP meetings?

‚ñº

Absolutely. Week 5 is dedicated specifically to IEP navigation, and you'll learn:

  • Your legal rights under IDEA
  • How to read and understand IEP documents
  • What questions to ask (and when to ask them)
  • How to advocate effectively without burning bridges
  • When to push back‚Äîand when to compromise
  • How to document progress and concerns

Parents consistently report feeling more confident, prepared, and collaborative in IEP meetings after taking this course.

What if my child is nonverbal?

‚ñº

This course is specifically designed with nonverbal children in mind. Communication is communication‚Äîwhether through words, sign language, PECS, or AAC devices.

You'll learn:

  • Alternative communication methods (PECS, sign language, AAC)
  • How to teach basic needs communication without verbal speech
  • Visual supports and strategies that work
  • How to advocate for speech/communication services in IEP meetings

The instructor has extensive experience working with nonverbal children and their families.

Is this course only for children with autism?

‚ñº

While the course was designed with autism in mind, the principles and strategies apply to any child with developmental delays, communication challenges, or behavioral needs.

Parents of children with ADHD, intellectual disabilities, speech delays, sensory processing disorders, and other diagnoses have found tremendous value in this course.

The foundational skills (communication, boundaries, following instructions, self-regulation, and independence) are universal‚Äîevery child benefits from these.

What ages is this appropriate for?

‚ñº

The course content is most relevant for children ages 2-12, but the principles apply across all ages.

  • Early Childhood (2-5): Perfect timing for foundational skills and early intervention navigation
  • Elementary (6-12): Focus on IEP advocacy, behavior management, and school partnership
  • Older Children (13+): Adapt strategies for self-advocacy, transition planning, and independence

Many strategies can be modified for developmental age rather than chronological age.
