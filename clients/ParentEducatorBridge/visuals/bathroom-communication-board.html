  • Start Early: Introduce these symbols 2-3 months before potty training begins

üí° Teaching Tips & Strategies

üè† At Home

  • Post the main board at child's eye level in the bathroom
  • Keep a portable card in the living room and car
  • Practice pointing to symbols during diaper changes
  • Use a timer - every 2 hours, guide child to board
  • Reward communication attempts (stickers, praise)
  • Read potty books and point to matching symbols
  • Let child decorate their own portable card

üè´ Sending to School

  • Laminate 2 portable cards (one backup)
  • Attach card to child's backpack or cubby
  • Email teacher a photo of the symbols you use at home
  • Schedule brief meeting to explain communication system
  • Request daily feedback on bathroom communication
  • Provide extra clothes and wipes in labeled bag
  • Ask teacher to use same words and praise system

‚ö° Quick Communication

  • Urgent: Red bordered cards = immediate response needed
  • Consistent Words: Use exact same phrases every time
  • Visual + Verbal: Always say the words while pointing
  • 30-Second Rule: Respond within 30 seconds of communication
  • No Punishment: Never scold for accidents, praise communication
  • Practice Daily: 10-15 repetitions minimum
  • Celebrate Small Wins: Pointing is success, even without potty use

üéØ School Readiness Goals

  • 3 Months Before: Child can point to "I need potty" symbol
  • 2 Months Before: Child uses symbol 3+ times daily
  • 1 Month Before: Child communicates BEFORE accident 50% of time
  • School Start: Child can use portable card independently
  • Backup Plan: Teacher knows symbols for non-verbal days
  • Success Metric: 80% communication rate (not 100% dry)
  • Parent-Teacher Check-in: Weekly updates first month

üö® Troubleshooting Common Challenges

  • Child won't use symbols: Start with favorite activities (snack, play) to teach pointing for requests
  • Too many accidents: Increase timer frequency to every 90 minutes, reduce liquid intake before school
  • Refuses help at school: Practice "asking teacher" role-play at home with stuffed animals
  • Verbal regression: Symbols provide backup - celebrate any communication method
  • Inconsistent success: Keep detailed log (time, success/accident) to find patterns
  • Teacher doesn't understand: Provide this printed guide and request IEP/504 accommodation if needed
  • Sensory issues with bathroom: Visit school bathroom during off-hours to practice
  • Child embarrassed by symbols: Let them pick "cool" stickers to personalize their card
