üìñ Parent Guide: Using the First-Then Board Effectively

What is First-Then?

The First-Then board is a visual support tool based on the Premack Principle (also called "Grandma's Rule"). This evidence-based strategy states that a less preferred activity (low-probability behavior) can be reinforced by following it with a more preferred activity (high-probability behavior).

In simple terms: "First you do this (less preferred), THEN you get this (more preferred)."

How First-Then Works (Premack Principle Explained)

The Premack Principle, discovered by psychologist David Premack, demonstrates that behaviors a child performs frequently and enjoys can serve as reinforcers for behaviors they perform less frequently or find less enjoyable.

The Science: By making access to a preferred activity contingent on completing a non-preferred activity, you increase the likelihood that the child will complete the non-preferred task. This creates a positive association and builds intrinsic motivation over time.

When to Use First-Then

  • Transitions: Moving between activities (e.g., from playtime to homework)
  • Task Motivation: Encouraging completion of non-preferred tasks
  • Routine Building: Establishing daily routines and expectations
  • Behavior Support: Reducing resistance to necessary activities
  • Independence Building: Teaching self-management and delayed gratification

Tips for Choosing Effective Rewards ("Then" Activities)

Key Principle: The "Then" reward MUST be something the child genuinely values and is motivated by in that moment. What works as a reward can change day to day.
  • Individualize: Choose rewards based on your child's unique interests and preferences
  • Immediate Access: The reward should be available immediately after task completion
  • Age-Appropriate: Select rewards that match developmental level
  • Vary Rewards: Rotate rewards to maintain motivation and prevent satiation
  • Natural Consequences: When possible, use logical rewards (e.g., "First clean up toys, then we can play a new game")
  • Time-Limit Rewards: Specify duration for time-based rewards (e.g., "10 minutes of iPad time")
  • High-Value Items: Save special rewards for challenging tasks

How to Implement First-Then Successfully

  1. Prepare the Board: Place the "First" and "Then" cards on the board before presenting it
  2. Present Clearly: Show the board and state: "First [task], THEN [reward]"
  3. Point to Cards: Use gestures to emphasize the sequence
  4. Wait for Task Completion: Ensure the "First" activity is fully completed before providing the "Then" reward
  5. Deliver Immediately: Provide the reward right after task completion
  6. Praise Effort: Acknowledge completion with specific praise
  7. Be Consistent: Always follow through - if you say "Then," you must deliver

Common Mistakes to Avoid

‚ö†Ô∏è Critical Errors That Reduce Effectiveness:
  • Giving the "Then" Early: Never provide the reward before the task is complete - this breaks the contingency
  • Using Punishers as "Then": The "Then" must ALWAYS be something positive and preferred
  • Unclear Completion Criteria: The child must know exactly what "done" looks like
  • Choosing Weak Rewards: If the "Then" isn't motivating, the system won't work
  • Inconsistent Follow-Through: Always deliver what you promise
  • Using First-Then as Bribery: Introduce First-Then before the problem behavior occurs, not during a meltdown
  • Making "First" Too Long: Start with short, achievable tasks and gradually increase duration

Assembly Instructions

  1. Print: Print boards and cards on heavy cardstock (110lb recommended)
  2. Laminate: Laminate all pieces for durability (optional but highly recommended)
  3. Apply Velcro: Attach hook velcro to board placeholders and loop velcro to backs of cards
  4. Cut Cards: Cut out individual task cards along borders
  5. Organize: Store cards in labeled containers or ziplock bags by category
  6. Create Portable Kit: Laminate portable board and store with cards in a small binder or pouch

Advanced Strategies

  • Graduated First-Then: Chain multiple "First" tasks before the final "Then" reward
  • First-Then Schedule: Create a visual schedule with multiple First-Then sequences for the day
  • Child Choice: Let child choose their "Then" reward from 2-3 options to increase buy-in
  • Self-Monitoring: Teach child to move cards themselves to build independence
  • Fading Support: Gradually reduce visual support as child internalizes expectations
üí° Remember: The First-Then board is a teaching tool, not a permanent solution. As your child develops self-regulation skills and intrinsic motivation, you can gradually fade the use of the board. The goal is to build internal motivation and understanding of task sequences, not create permanent dependence on external rewards.
