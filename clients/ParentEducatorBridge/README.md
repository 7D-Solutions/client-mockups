# ParentEducatorBridge - Course Development Project

## Overview
A 6-week parenting course for children with autism, designed to bridge the gap between parents and educators. Created by an experienced School Psychologist and Licensed Professional Counselor with 35+ years of experience.

## Project Vision
**Mission:** Help parents understand how special education actually works, prepare their children with foundational skills, and become effective advocates who partner with schools rather than battle them.

**Core Message:** "The 5 Things I Wish Someone Had Told Me as a Parent"

## Files in This Project

### 1. COURSE_MOCKUP.md
Complete 6-week curriculum including:
- Course philosophy and problem statement
- "Before School Can Work" foundational skills framework
- Detailed weekly curriculum outlines
- Delivery options and pricing
- Marketing messages and target audience
- Financial projections
- Development roadmap

### 2. FOUNDATIONAL_SKILLS_HANDOUT.md
Parent handout covering the 5 critical skills children need before school can be effective:
1. Basic Communication of Needs (bathroom, help, break)
2. Boundaries & Saying "No"
3. Following Basic Instructions
4. Basic Self-Regulation
5. Foundational Independence

Includes teaching strategies, practice scripts, and assessment checklist.

### 3. FIVE_THINGS_QUICKCARD.md
Quick reference card summarizing:
- School can't replace home teaching
- 1:1 support isn't always the answer
- "Appropriate" doesn't mean "optimal"
- Behavior is communication
- Parents and schools are on the same team

Plus helpful phrases for IEP meetings.

### 4. IEP_PREP_WORKSHEET.md
Comprehensive IEP meeting preparation tool:
- Pre-meeting checklist and documentation
- Parent rights summary
- Questions to ask in each area
- During-meeting notes template
- After-meeting follow-up tasks
- "About My Child" one-pager template
- Phrases for when you disagree

### 5. SERVICE_CONTINUUM_EXPLAINED.md
Educational document explaining:
- The full continuum of services (Levels 1-8)
- Least Restrictive Environment (LRE) requirements
- When 1:1 support is/isn't appropriate
- Questions to ask about placement
- The importance of independence as a goal

## Key Differentiators

1. **Experience-Based:** 35 years on BOTH sides (educator AND parent advocate)
2. **Realistic:** Honest about what schools can/can't do
3. **Skills-Focused:** Emphasizes parent responsibility for foundational skills
4. **Partnership-Oriented:** Builds collaboration, not adversarial relationships
5. **Evidence-Based:** Grounded in research and proven practices

## Target Audience

- Parents of newly diagnosed children with autism
- Parents struggling with the IEP process
- Parents whose children are transitioning to school
- Parents feeling overwhelmed and isolated
- Educators seeking parent perspectives (professional version)
