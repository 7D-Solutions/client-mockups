Before the Meeting

  • Review your child's current IEP and progress reports
  • Write down 3-5 specific concerns with examples
  • List what IS working (start positive!)
  • Prepare your "About My Child" one-pager
  • Bring any outside evaluations or doctor's notes
  • Know who will attend and their roles

Questions to Ask

  • "How is my child progressing toward their goals?"
  • "What data supports this recommendation?"
  • "What's the plan to fade supports over time?"
  • "What should I be working on at home?"
  • "When will we review if this is working?"

Your Rights to Remember

  • You are an EQUAL member of the IEP team
  • You can bring anyone you want to the meeting
  • You do NOT have to sign the IEP at the meeting
  • You can request "Prior Written Notice" for any denial

After the Meeting

  • Send follow-up email summarizing decisions
  • Review the IEP document carefully before signing
  • Calendar the next progress check date
  • Start documenting for next meeting
