Core Wisdom

The 5 Things I Wish Someone Had Told Me

After 35 years as a school psychologist, special education director, and counselor, these are the truths every parent needs to hear.

1

School Cannot Replace What You Teach at Home

You have 3x more teaching time than school. Foundational skills must be taught at home‚Äîrelentlessly, daily.

  • School = 1,080 hours/year
  • Home = 3,650+ hours/year
  • Communication, boundaries, and self-regulation start with YOU
  • No IEP can replace consistent home teaching
2

1:1 Support Isn't Always the Answer

More support can create dependency. Children need to learn from peers. Independence is the goal‚Äînot constant help.

  • 1:1 aides can prevent skill development
  • Children look to the aide instead of peers
  • Social isolation often increases
  • Ask: "How will we FADE this support over time?"
3

"Appropriate" Doesn't Mean "Optimal"

Schools must provide appropriate education‚Äînot the best possible. Focus on whether your child is making progress.

  • FAPE = Free Appropriate Public Education
  • You can't legally demand "the best"
  • Document when progress ISN'T happening
  • That's when you have a case
4

Behavior Is Communication

When your child "acts out," they're telling you something. Figure out WHAT‚Äîthen teach them a better way to say it.

  • "This is too hard" / "I don't understand"
  • "I'm overwhelmed" / "I need attention"
  • "I want to escape this"
  • ABC Model: Antecedent ‚Üí Behavior ‚Üí Consequence
5

You and the School Are on the Same Team

Most educators WANT to help. They're limited by resources and time‚Äînot caring. Partnership beats battle every time.

  • Assume good intent (until proven otherwise)
  • Ask questions before accusing
  • Bring solutions, not just problems
  • Save the fight for when it really matters

Communicate Basic Needs

‚úÖ What to Teach

  • "I need to go to the bathroom" / "Potty"
  • "I need help" / "Help me"
  • "I don't understand"
  • "I want ___" (basic requests)
  • "I'm done" / "All done"
  • "I need a break"

üõ†Ô∏è How to Teach It

  • Choose ANY method: verbal, sign, PECS, AAC device
  • Create opportunities‚Äîdon't anticipate every need
  • Prompt ‚Üí Response ‚Üí Immediate reward
  • Practice 10+ times daily
  • Generalize: different people, places, times
üõë

Boundaries & Saying "No"

‚úÖ What to Teach

  • Can say/sign/indicate "No" or "Stop"
  • Pushes away unwanted items or touch
  • Shows discomfort with unfamiliar people
  • Can identify 3-5 "safe" trusted adults
  • Understands basic body boundaries

üõ†Ô∏è How to Teach It

  • When your child says "no"‚ÄîHONOR IT
  • Tickle games: "Say STOP" ‚Üí immediately stop
  • Create a photo board of safe people
  • Teach body autonomy: "Your body belongs to YOU"
  • Show them their words have POWER
üëÇ

Following Basic Instructions

‚úÖ What to Teach

  • Responds to their name
  • Follows one-step directions: "Sit down," "Come here"
  • Stops when told "Stop" or "Wait"
  • Can transition between activities with support
  • Can sit in a designated spot for 2-3 minutes

üõ†Ô∏è How to Teach It

  • Start simple: one-word commands
  • The 3-Second Rule: Give direction ‚Üí Wait 3 sec ‚Üí Guide if needed ‚Üí Reward
  • Never give a direction you can't enforce
  • Use visual timers for transitions
  • "First ___, then ___" structure
üòå

Basic Self-Regulation

‚úÖ What to Teach

  • Shows early warning signs of overwhelm
  • Accepts help when upset
  • Uses at least one calming strategy with prompting
  • Can recover from upset in reasonable time
  • Can wait briefly for something they want

üõ†Ô∏è How to Teach It

  • Learn YOUR child's warning signs
  • Build a calming toolkit (practice when CALM)
  • Deep breaths, squeeze balls, weighted blanket
  • Practice waiting: start with 5 seconds
  • Don't try to teach DURING meltdowns
üö∂

Foundational Independence

‚úÖ What to Teach

  • Toilet trained OR can communicate needs
  • Can eat provided food
  • Can separate from parent without extreme distress
  • Can sit at a table for short periods
  • Can be in a room with other children

üõ†Ô∏è How to Teach It

  • Toileting is #1 issue‚Äîwork on this intensively at home
  • Practice independent play: start with 30 seconds
  • Consistent goodbye routine‚Äîdon't sneak out
  • Playdates, library time, community activities
  • Gradually increase duration and newness
